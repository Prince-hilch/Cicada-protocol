About

Cicada Protocol is a conceptual cybersecurity architecture designed for state-level institutional environments where failure is not an option election bodies, government ministries, central banks, national health systems, and critical infrastructure operators.

Built entirely around a single design principle: assume the attacker is already inside.

Most security systems are built like walls. Cicada Protocol is built like an immune system. It does not try to keep attackers out. It makes sure that getting in never becomes staying in, staying in never becomes stable ground, and stable ground never becomes damage.

This repository contains the complete system architecture documentation, an interactive browser-based prototype demonstrating all seven security layers, and the full design philosophy behind every decision.

The Seven Layers

Layer 1 — Drift Engine
Continuous behavioral identity monitoring. Every user builds a behavioral fingerprint over time login patterns, typing cadence, file access sequences, navigation habits, geographic consistency. Authentication proves you have the right key. The Drift Engine proves you are the right person. Any session that passes authentication but deviates from the established behavioral fingerprint is silently sandboxed the attacker continues operating inside a monitored container where no action causes real damage, while analysts are alerted in the background.

Layer 2 — Decay Protocol
Time-based privilege degradation. No access right inside Cicada Protocol lasts forever. Emergency access expires in 2 hours. Administrative access expires in 72 hours. Elevated access expires in 7 days. Standard access expires in 30 days. Credentials that are stolen but not immediately used provide a progressively narrowing window of opportunity regardless of whether the theft has been detected. Time catches attackers that detection misses.

Layer 3 — Phantom Topology
Moving target defense infrastructure. The institution's network never holds still long enough to be fully and accurately mapped. Network pathways rotate on randomized intervals. Server container identities regenerate regularly. Internal addresses shift. Backdoors planted in the infrastructure point to addresses that no longer exist by the time the attacker is ready to use them. Reconnaissance becomes worthless because the environment changes faster than it can be mapped.

Layer 4 — Collective Immunity Grid
Cross-institutional threat intelligence network. Every Cicada Protocol installation participates in a cryptographically secured, anonymized threat intelligence mesh. When any node detects a credible threat, the signature is extracted, stripped of identifying information, and broadcast to all connected institutions within 15 minutes. An attack technique that works against one institution stops working against all institutions almost immediately after first use.

Layer 5 — Regenerative Response Engine
Autonomous containment and recovery. When a severe incident is confirmed across multiple independent detection signals, containment executes in seconds without waiting for human authorization. Affected network segments are isolated surgically. Encryption keys rotate instantly. Clean replacement instances spin up for critical services. The institution never goes offline. Human authority governs recovery decisions. Automation governs the containment speed that makes recovery possible.

Layer 6 — Forensic Chain
Tamper-proof audit logging. Every action within the institution is recorded in an append-only, cryptographically chained log where any modification of any historical entry invalidates the hash chain from that point forward. Logs are continuously replicated to geographically distributed storage nodes isolated from the operational environment. A parallel Dual-Path Forensics system uses two independently developed engines to reconstruct event timelines if their outputs diverge, the divergence itself is flagged as evidence of compromise.

Layer 7 — Obsidian Protocol
Last resort survival mechanism. Activates only when all seven layers are simultaneously and conclusively compromised. Executes four phases in parallel: all sensitive data is fragmented and distributed across geographically separated nodes requiring physical key holders to reconstruct. The entire infrastructure undergoes full emergency mutation simultaneously, destroying every map, backdoor, and persistent access the attacker established. An air-gapped sovereign backup environment comes online at a separate physical facility. The complete forensic chain is broadcast irrevocably to three independent receivers a national cybersecurity authority, an independent legal body, and an encrypted dead-drop before the attacker has any opportunity to prevent it. The attacker leaves with nothing. The institution never goes offline.

Design Philosophy

The core insight behind Cicada Protocol is that perfect prevention is impossible and that every security architecture built on the assumption that it is will eventually fail catastrophically.

The alternative is not pessimism. It is honest architecture. Build systems that assume breach. Build systems where breach does not become victory. Build systems where the attacker's advantages against a static target become costs against a dynamic one.

Cicada Protocol borrows its design philosophy entirely from biology. A human immune system does not maintain a list of every pathogen that exists. It builds a deep model of what healthy looks like and treats deviation from that model as the threat signal. It responds proportionally. It contains. It adapts. It remembers. It never shuts the body down to do its job.

Every layer of Cicada Protocol operates on this principle.

The Five Paradoxes

Any honest security architecture has to confront the philosophical tensions it creates. Cicada Protocol identifies five explicitly.

The Adaptation Paradox — the stronger the system becomes at adapting, the less explainable its behavior becomes. Resolved through the Transparent Mutation Ledger: every automated action is documented in plain language accessible to non-technical oversight bodies.

The Sovereignty Paradox — a system that can autonomously rebuild national infrastructure raises the question of who actually holds authority over that infrastructure. Resolved through the Constitutional Trigger Doctrine: Obsidian Protocol's activation conditions are defined in law before deployment, making automated execution the execution of democratically authorized instructions, not a machine decision.

The Trust Paradox — continuous behavioral monitoring of officials signals that they are not trusted. Resolved through Consent Architecture and Visibility Inversion: every monitored individual explicitly consents, and has full visibility into their own behavioral score and any flags against their account.

The Immunity Paradox — if cyber attacks become prohibitively difficult, adversaries shift to political pressure, influence operations, and physical coercion. Resolved through the Hybrid Threat Doctrine: Cicada Protocol acknowledges it solves the cyber dimension only, while noting that behavioral monitoring data may surface early indicators of non-cyber threat campaigns.

The Complacency Paradox — if the system works for years without a catastrophic breach, institutional leadership concludes they are invincible, and that belief becomes the new vulnerability. Resolved through the Mandatory Institutional Humility Protocol: the Resilience Score never displays above 94, quarterly red-team exercises are legally required, and controlled simulated attacks are designed to partially succeed so organizational memory of failure never fades.

Deployment Tiers

Essential Mode — Drift Engine, Decay Protocol, basic Regenerative Response. Connects to the national Collective Immunity Grid without requiring local Grid infrastructure. Appropriate for regional offices, municipal authorities, district-level election administration.

Advanced Mode — adds Phantom Topology, Digital Twin simulation environment, Dual-Path Forensics, and the full dashboard. Appropriate for national ministry departments, major hospital networks, regional central bank branches.

Sovereign Mode — complete system including full Collective Immunity Grid with Fractal Consensus Protocol, Obsidian Protocol with Phoenix Threshold Mechanism, Geographic Key Distribution, and Constitutional Trigger Doctrine integration. Appropriate for national election bodies, central banking infrastructure, heads of state and senior cabinet officials.
